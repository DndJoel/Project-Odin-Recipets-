Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish of pasta sheets layered with fillings and baked until hot and bubbly. The word "lasagna" comes from the Latin word lasanum, which means "cooking pot" or "chamber pot".

Ingredients
  • 2 pounds (1 kg) fresh lasagna noodles
  • 1/2 cup (150 ml) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup (150 ml) heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup (150 ml) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup (150 ml) fresh mozzarella cheese, grated
  • 1/2 cup (150 ml) grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 1/2 cup (150 ml) grated Romano cheese
  • 1/2 cup (150 ml) grated Fontina cheese

Instructions
  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (225°C).

  2. Cut the lasagna noodles into 1-inch (2.5 cm) cubes.

  3. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the noodles and cook for 2-3 minutes, until they're al dente.

  4. Add the heavy cream, Parmesan, mozzarella, Parmigiano-Reggiano, Romano, and Fontina cheeses, and stir well to combine.

  5. Add the cooked lasagna noodles to the skillet and toss to combine.

  6. Serve the lasagna over a bed of alfredo sauce, sprinkled with fresh parsley
